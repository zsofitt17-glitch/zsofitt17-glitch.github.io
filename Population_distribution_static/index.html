Population Distribution by Day and Time

This visualization compares the dynamic population distribution of Helsinki across different parts of the day using a 250 metre statistical grid. The 3×3 layout shows weekdays, Saturdays and Sundays at 01:00, 09:00 and 19:00, making it possible to compare nighttime, daytime and evening population patterns side by side.

Although the differences between the selected hours are visible, they are not extremely sharp, since many of the areas with a high number of workplaces also contain a high number of dwellings, especially in and around the city centre. This overlap can also be seen in the postal code visualizations in the Postal code section, where the same central areas show both dense residential and workplace concentrations. In the outer parts of the city, residential buildings are more common, but they generally contain fewer dwellings than the apartment-dense areas closer to the centre.

For this reason, the static maps should be understood mainly as a tool for comparing selected moments of the day, while the actual movement of population is easier to observe in the interactive animated version.
